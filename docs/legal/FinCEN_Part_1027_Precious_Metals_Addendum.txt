1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

This Addendum supplements the AurumShield BSA/AML Compliance Program to address
the specific obligations imposed on PVN LLC d/b/a AurumShield under FinCEN
31 CFR Part 1027 — the rule governing Dealers in Precious Metals, Precious
Stones, or Jewels ("PMSJs").

This document establishes binding institutional policy for all AurumShield
employees, contractors, registered brokers, and third-party agents involved in
the clearing, settlement, custody, or physical handling of precious metals.

Non-compliance with any provision of this Addendum constitutes grounds for
immediate termination and referral for criminal prosecution.

================================================================================
2. APPLICABILITY — PMSJ CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

2.1 Covered Dealer Status

PVN LLC d/b/a AurumShield operates as a covered dealer under 31 CFR
§ 1027.100(b). A "dealer" is defined as any person engaged in the business of
purchasing and selling precious metals, precious stones, or jewels who, during
the prior calendar year, purchased and sold more than $50,000 in covered goods.

AurumShield's annual transaction volume in physical gold clearing and settlement
far exceeds this threshold. Accordingly, AurumShield is subject to the full
regulatory obligations of Part 1027, including but not limited to:

    • AML Program Requirements (§ 1027.210)
    • Suspicious Activity Reporting (§ 1027.320)
    • Recordkeeping Requirements (31 CFR § 1010.410)
    • IRS/FinCEN Form 8300 Reporting (26 U.S.C. § 6050I)

2.2 Scope of Covered Goods

For purposes of this Addendum, "precious metals" includes gold, silver,
platinum, and palladium in any form — including but not limited to:

    • Refined bars (LBMA Good Delivery, kilobars, tola bars)
    • Coins and rounds (sovereign mint and private mint)
    • Unrefined forms (Doré, scrap, granules, sponge)
    • Allocated and unallocated vault positions representing physical metal

2.3 Institutional Obligation

All AurumShield personnel must understand that PMSJ obligations exist in
addition to — not in lieu of — the MSB obligations set forth in the parent
BSA/AML Compliance Program. The Part 1027 requirements impose specialized
physical supply chain due diligence that does not apply to purely financial
transactions.

================================================================================
3. SUPPLY CHAIN DUE DILIGENCE
================================================================================

3.1 Mandate

No physical gold may enter, exit, or be transferred within AurumShield's
clearing ledger, custody chain, or settlement pipeline unless the supply chain
due diligence requirements set forth in this Section have been satisfied in
their entirety.

3.2 Chain of Custody Verification

AurumShield requires unbroken Chain of Custody ("CoC") documentation for all
inbound physical metal. CoC documentation must trace every transfer of physical
metal from the producing refinery (or mine of origin) to the point of delivery
to AurumShield's custodian or vault partner.

Required documentation at each node of the custody chain:

    • Transport manifests identifying carrier, origin, destination, and seal
      numbers
    • Vault transfer receipts with counterparty signatures
    • Handover confirmations with date, time, and responsible party
      identification
    • Tamper-evident seal verification at each transfer point

Any gap, discrepancy, or unexplained break in the CoC is a critical compliance
failure. Metal with broken CoC documentation must be:

    (a) Immediately quarantined and excluded from the clearing ledger;
    (b) Escalated to the Compliance Officer within 24 hours;
    (c) Evaluated for SAR filing under § 1027.320.

3.3 Assay Authentication

Every physical gold bar entering AurumShield's custody or clearing ledger must
be accompanied by a valid Assay Certificate confirming:

    • Refinery name and LBMA accreditation status
    • Bar serial number matching the physical hallmark
    • Gross weight in troy ounces or grams
    • Fineness (minimum 995.0 parts per thousand for gold)
    • Date of assay and certifying metallurgist signature

Assay certificates must be original documents bearing the refinery's official
seal. Photocopies, digital screenshots, and unsigned certificates are not
acceptable without independent verification from the issuing refinery.

AurumShield accepts assay certificates exclusively from:

    • LBMA Good Delivery List refineries
    • Dubai Good Delivery List refineries (DGCX)
    • Partner refineries approved by AurumShield's Compliance Committee

Discrepancies between the physical hallmark and the assay certificate — in
serial number, weight, or fineness — are grounds for immediate rejection of the
bar and escalation for potential SAR filing.

3.4 Prohibition on Conflict Minerals

AurumShield categorically prohibits the acceptance, clearing, settlement,
custody, or facilitation of:

    (a) Conflict gold — metal originating from conflict-affected or high-risk
        areas ("CAHRA") as defined by the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for
        Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and
        High-Risk Areas, including but not limited to artisanal mining regions
        in:
            • Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)
            • Republic of Sudan
            • Central African Republic (CAR)
            • Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela
            • Republic of Mali

    (b) Sanctioned-origin gold — metal originating from or refined in:
            • The Russian Federation (gold refined on or after March 7, 2022,
              per Executive Order 14024 and OFAC Directive 4 under E.O. 14024)
            • The Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK)
            • The Islamic Republic of Iran
            • The Syrian Arab Republic
            • Republic of Cuba
            • Russian-occupied Crimea, Donetsk, and Luhansk regions of Ukraine

3.5 OECD 5-Step Due Diligence Framework

AurumShield implements the OECD five-step framework for responsible mineral
supply chains:

    Step 1:  Establish strong company management systems for supply chain due
             diligence.
    Step 2:  Identify and assess risks in the supply chain.
    Step 3:  Design and implement a strategy to respond to identified risks.
    Step 4:  Carry out independent third-party audit of supply chain due
             diligence.
    Step 5:  Report annually on supply chain due diligence.

The Compliance Officer is responsible for maintaining documented evidence of
AurumShield's compliance with each of these five steps.

4. PHYSICAL METAL TYPOLOGIES — RED FLAGS
================================================================================

4.1 Mandate

AurumShield's Suspicious Metal Logistics ("SML") engine and all compliance
personnel must monitor for the following red flags specific to trade-based money
laundering ("TBML") through physical precious metals. Detection of any red flag
requires immediate escalation and SAR evaluation.

4.2 Altered Hallmarks

    • Bars exhibiting hand-stamped, re-engraved, partially polished, or
      otherwise altered refinery hallmarks
    • Mismatched serial numbers between the physical bar and accompanying assay
      certificate
    • Hallmarks from refineries not on the LBMA Good Delivery List, the Dubai
      Good Delivery List, or AurumShield's approved refinery register
    • Bars that appear to have been re-cast, re-melted, or re-stamped to
      conceal original provenance markings

4.3 Non-Standard Product Forms

    • Transactions involving unrefined scrap gold, Doré bars, granules, or
      sponge that deviate from standard LBMA 400 oz or 1 kg bar formats
    • Metal presented without standardized refinery packaging, protective
      casing, or tamper-evident seals
    • Bars of irregular weight, non-standard dimensions, or unusual alloy
      composition inconsistent with declared fineness
    • Metal bearing hallmarks from entities not recognized as refineries
      (e.g., jewelry manufacturers, pawn operations, recycling facilities)

4.4 Behavioral Indicators

    • Customers who exhibit unusual lack of concern for storage fees, insurance
      premiums, vaulting costs, or purity testing — legitimate gold investors
      are highly sensitive to these economics
    • Buyers who pay significant premiums above spot price without negotiation,
      suggesting the transaction purpose is value transfer rather than
      investment
    • Customers who purchase refined gold and immediately request re-smelting
      or re-assay at a different refinery ("rapid buy-refine cycle") — a
      technique to launder provenance by creating new documentation
    • Requests for immediate physical delivery to a third-party address,
      particularly to non-standard locations (e.g., residential addresses,
      freight forwarding agents, free trade zones)
    • Customers who are unusually knowledgeable about SAR thresholds, reporting
      requirements, or who ask whether transactions are reported to the
      government
    • Profile inconsistencies — a small trading company with modest declared
      revenue placing orders inconsistent with its stated business capacity

4.5 Structured Cash Purchases

    • Multiple cash or cash-equivalent transactions from the same customer (or
      related customers) that aggregate to exceed $10,000 within a 24-hour
      period or over consecutive business days
    • Transactions deliberately structured to remain below $10,000 per
      individual receipt, with amounts such as $9,500, $9,800, or $9,900
      indicating deliberate threshold avoidance
    • Use of multiple cashier's checks, money orders, or bank drafts with face
      values at or near $10,000 to fund a single purchase

Structuring is a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324 regardless of whether the
underlying funds are legitimate.

5. FORM 8300 ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

5.1 Legal Basis

Under 26 U.S.C. § 6050I and 31 CFR § 1010.330, any person engaged in a trade
or business who receives more than $10,000 in cash or cash equivalents in a
single transaction — or in two or more related transactions within a 24-hour
period — must file IRS/FinCEN Form 8300 ("Report of Cash Payments Over $10,000
Received in a Trade or Business").

5.2 Definition of Cash and Cash Equivalents

For purposes of Form 8300 reporting, "cash" includes:

    • U.S. and foreign currency (coins and paper money)
    • Cashier's checks with a face value of $10,000 or less
    • Bank drafts with a face value of $10,000 or less
    • Traveler's checks with a face value of $10,000 or less
    • Money orders with a face value of $10,000 or less

A cashier's check, bank draft, traveler's check, or money order with a face
value exceeding $10,000 is NOT treated as "cash" for Form 8300 purposes — but
its receipt may still trigger other BSA reporting obligations.

Wire transfers and ACH transactions are NOT cash or cash equivalents for
Form 8300 purposes.

5.3 Aggregation Rule

AurumShield personnel must aggregate multiple cash payments from the same
buyer (or the buyer's agent) that the dealer knows — or has reason to know —
are related. Related transactions include:

    • Multiple payments toward a single purchase
    • Multiple payments made on the same day
    • Multiple payments made on consecutive business days where the pattern
      indicates a single transaction or plan

Example: A customer pays $6,000 in cash on Monday and $6,000 in cash on
Tuesday toward the same gold purchase. The aggregate ($12,000) exceeds $10,000.
Form 8300 must be filed.

5.4 Filing Procedures

    (a) Detection:  AurumShield's settlement engine automatically flags any
                    transaction where the settlement rail is classified as a
                    cash equivalent AND the settlement amount exceeds $10,000.
                    The settlement status is locked to "FORM_8300_REQUIRED" and
                    cannot proceed until cleared by the Compliance Officer.

    (b) Filing:     Form 8300 must be filed with both FinCEN and the IRS within
                    fifteen (15) calendar days of the date of receipt of the
                    cash or cash-equivalent payment.

    (c) Payor       A written statement must be provided to the payor by
        Notice:     January 31 of the calendar year following the year in which
                    the cash was received, notifying the payor that Form 8300
                    was filed.

    (d) Retention:  A copy of each filed Form 8300 and all supporting
                    documentation must be retained for five (5) years from the
                    date of filing.

5.5 Penalties for Non-Compliance

    • Civil penalty:    Minimum $25,000 per violation for failure to file
    • Criminal penalty: Up to $250,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment for
                        willful failure to file (26 U.S.C. § 7206)
    • Structuring:      Up to $500,000 fine and/or 10 years imprisonment
                        (31 U.S.C. § 5324)

5.6 Interaction with SAR Obligations

Form 8300 filing does NOT satisfy SAR obligations. If the cash transaction also
involves suspicious indicators (e.g., suspected structuring, profile
inconsistency, red flags identified in Section 4), a SAR must be filed
independently within 30 days under § 1027.320.

Conversely, SAR filing does NOT satisfy Form 8300 obligations. Both filings are
independently mandatory when their respective criteria are met.

6. COMPLIANCE OFFICER ATTESTATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

6.1 Physical Metals Due Diligence Gate

Before approving any settlement, custody transfer, or title transfer involving
physical precious metals, the designated Compliance Officer (or authorized
delegate) must complete and attest to the following three mandatory due
diligence verifications:

    [ ] CHAIN OF CUSTODY VERIFIED
        "I attest that the origin of this metal has been traced to a
        non-sanctioned, OECD-compliant source, and that unbroken Chain of
        Custody documentation has been reviewed and verified."

    [ ] ASSAY AUTHENTICITY CONFIRMED
        "I attest that the refiner hallmarks and assay certificates have been
        validated against the LBMA Good Delivery List, the Dubai Good Delivery
        List, or AurumShield's approved partner refinery register."

    [ ] FORM 8300 APPLICABILITY REVIEWED
        "I have verified that no single or related cash-equivalent deposits
        exceeding $10,000 were used to fund this transaction, OR the required
        Form 8300 has been filed and a copy retained in the compliance archive."

No settlement or title transfer may proceed without all three attestations
recorded in AurumShield's immutable Governance Audit Log.

6.2 Audit Trail

Each attestation must be logged with:

    • Identity of the attesting Compliance Officer
    • Timestamp of attestation (UTC)
    • Case or settlement identifier
    • Counterparty identifier
    • Metal description (weight, fineness, serial numbers, refinery)
    • Cryptographic certificate ID (if training certification is applicable)

7. EMPLOYEE TRAINING
================================================================================

7.1 Annual Certification

All AurumShield personnel involved in precious metals operations must complete
annual FinCEN Part 1027 compliance training. The training curriculum must
include:

    Module 1:   Part 1027 & Form 8300 — PMSJ classification, cash-equivalent
                definitions, aggregation rules, 15-day filing window, penalties.

    Module 2:   Supply Chain Due Diligence — LBMA Good Delivery verification,
                assay certificate authentication, Chain of Custody requirements,
                conflict gold and sanctioned-origin prohibitions, OECD 5-Step
                Framework.

    Module 3:   Physical Metal Typologies — Trade-Based Money Laundering red
                flags, altered hallmarks, scrap/Doré deviations, behavioral
                indicators, rapid buy-refine cycles.

    Module 4:   SARs & Tipping Off — SAR filing obligations for physical metal
                transactions (§ 1027.320), 30-day timeline, $5,000 aggregation
                threshold, tipping-off prohibition (31 U.S.C. § 5318(g)(2)),
                narrative quality standards.

Training must conclude with a hard-stop knowledge check (quiz) for Modules 2
through 4 and a binding digital attestation under penalty of federal law.

Training completion and attestation records must be retained for five (5) years.

8. ENFORCEMENT AND DISCIPLINARY ACTION
================================================================================

8.1 Violations

Any AurumShield employee, contractor, registered broker, or agent who:

    (a) Accepts physical metal without completing required due diligence;
    (b) Falsifies or omits Chain of Custody documentation;
    (c) Fails to file Form 8300 when required;
    (d) Structures or assists in structuring transactions to evade reporting;
    (e) Discloses SAR-related information in violation of the tipping-off
        prohibition; or
    (f) Approves a settlement without completing the Physical Metals Due
        Diligence attestation

shall be subject to:

    • Immediate suspension of platform access
    • Termination of employment or contractual relationship
    • Referral to FinCEN, IRS, and/or law enforcement as applicable
    • Personal criminal liability under applicable federal statutes

8.2 Corporate Liability

PVN LLC acknowledges that willful violations of Part 1027, Form 8300 reporting
obligations, or SAR filing requirements expose the entity to:

    • Civil money penalties up to $500,000 per violation
    • Criminal fines up to $1,000,000 per violation under IEEPA
    • Imprisonment of responsible individuals up to 20 years (IEEPA)
    • Debarment from the U.S. financial system
    • Reputational destruction incompatible with continued operations
