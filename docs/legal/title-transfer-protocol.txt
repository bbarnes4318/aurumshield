DOCUMENT V
AURUMSHIELD BULLION TITLE TRANSFER PROTOCOL

Network: AurumShield Institutional Settlement Network
Operator: PVN LLC
Version: 1.0

1. PURPOSE

This Bullion Title Transfer Protocol (“Protocol”) establishes the legal and operational framework governing the transfer of ownership of allocated bullion within the AurumShield Network.

The Protocol ensures that all bullion transfers occur through a deterministic Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) mechanism ensuring simultaneous transfer of value and title.

2. LEGAL STRUCTURE OF TITLE

Bullion held through AurumShield is maintained in allocated form at approved vault providers.

Legal ownership of each bar is assigned to a specific participant within the AurumShield ledger.

Title to bullion exists simultaneously in two forms:

Physical Title Record
Maintained by the vault provider inventory system.

Digital Ownership Record
Maintained by the AurumShield settlement ledger.

The AurumShield ledger constitutes the authoritative operational record for settlement transfers.

3. BAR IDENTIFICATION

Each bullion bar is identified by the following attributes:

• serial number
• refiner
• weight
• purity
• vault location

These attributes form the unique bar identity record.

4. DIGITAL TITLE REPRESENTATION

For operational efficiency, AurumShield represents ownership of bullion bars through ledger-based digital title entries.

Each entry represents a participant’s entitlement to specific bullion bars stored in vault custody.

Digital title entries may represent:

• whole bars
• fractional bar interests

Fractional ownership may occur when bars are partially transferred between counterparties.

5. TRANSFER INITIATION

A bullion transfer is initiated when a participant submits a Settlement Instruction through the AurumShield platform.

The instruction must specify:

• counterparty identifier
• quantity of bullion
• settlement price
• settlement asset

6. PRE-SETTLEMENT VALIDATION

Before settlement execution, the AurumShield system verifies:

• sufficient bullion balance
• sufficient settlement funds
• regulatory compliance checks
• counterparty eligibility

If any validation fails, the transfer request is rejected.

7. ASSET LOCK

Upon successful validation, the AurumShield system temporarily reserves the relevant bullion balance within the participant’s account.

Reserved balances cannot be used for other transactions.

8. DELIVERY-VERSUS-PAYMENT EXECUTION

Settlement occurs through the AurumShield deterministic DvP state machine.

The state machine performs the following actions simultaneously:

Transfers digital title of bullion from seller to buyer.

Transfers settlement value from buyer to seller.

The settlement asset may include:

• fiat funds received via Fedwire
• digital settlement assets such as USDC or USDT.

9. FINALIZATION

Once the DvP process completes, the transaction enters the Finalized State.

At this point:

• ownership records are updated
• the transfer becomes irreversible
• vault inventory attribution is updated

No participant or administrator can reverse the transaction once finalized.

10. VAULT RECORD RECONCILIATION

Periodic reconciliation is performed between:

• AurumShield digital title records
• vault provider physical inventory records

This ensures consistency between digital and physical ownership records.

11. PHYSICAL DELIVERY

Participants may request physical withdrawal of bullion.

Such withdrawals require:

• bar identification
• vault release authorization
• logistics coordination

Physical delivery requests are processed by the vault provider.

12. GOVERNING LAW

Title transfers executed through the AurumShield ledger are governed by Article 8 of the Uniform Commercial Code and the laws of the State of New York.
