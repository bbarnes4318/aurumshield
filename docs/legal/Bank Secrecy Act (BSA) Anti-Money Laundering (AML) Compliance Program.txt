1. Institutional Overview

AurumShield operates a high-value B2B settlement infrastructure enabling corporate counterparties to settle obligations using:

• Fiat bank wires
• Stablecoin settlement bridges (USDC / USDT)
• Allocated vaulted gold ownership transfers

The system operates as a deterministic Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) settlement mechanism.

Given the high monetary value of transactions, AurumShield implements a Fail-Closed Compliance Architecture, meaning:

No funds, digital assets, or metal title transfers may occur unless compliance clearance has been completed.

This program establishes the framework for:

Customer identity verification

Sanctions screening

Transaction monitoring

Suspicious activity reporting

Record retention

Independent compliance testing

2. Regulatory Framework

AurumShield operates under the regulatory requirements applicable to Money Services Businesses (MSBs) as defined in 31 CFR §1010.100(ff).

The company maintains compliance with:

FinCEN AML Program Requirements

Written internal controls

3. Compliance Governance
3.1 Board Oversight

The Board of Managers of PVN LLC maintains ultimate responsibility for AML compliance.

Responsibilities include:

Approving the AML program

Ensuring adequate resources

Reviewing compliance reports

Overseeing independent audits

3.2 Designation of BSA/AML Compliance Officer

AurumShield appoints a BSA/AML Compliance Officer responsible for administering the AML program.

The Compliance Officer reports directly to executive leadership and the Board.

Authority

The Compliance Officer possesses the authority to:

Halt settlements

Freeze platform accounts

Block onboarding

Escalate suspicious activity

File regulatory reports

Suspend counterparties

No business unit may override compliance decisions.

4. Risk-Based Compliance Framework

AurumShield implements a risk-based AML program as recommended by FinCEN guidance.

Risk exposure is evaluated across four dimensions:

Customer Risk

Factors include:

Corporate ownership complexity

Jurisdiction of incorporation

Regulatory status

Geographic Risk

Heightened scrutiny for entities connected to:

FATF grey/black list jurisdictions

Sanctioned jurisdictions

High-corruption environments

Transaction Risk

Risk indicators include:

Unusually large transaction sizes

Rapid asset transfers

Unexplained economic purpose

Product Risk

AurumShield supports multiple asset rails:

Asset Type	Risk Profile
Fiat wire transfers	Moderate
Stablecoin settlement	Elevated
Allocated gold transfers	Moderate
5. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

AurumShield maintains a Customer Identification Program in accordance with 31 CFR §1022.220.

The purpose of the CIP is to:

Establish customer identity

Verify beneficial ownership

Prevent anonymous access to financial infrastructure

No account may be activated without successful identity verification.

6. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) & KYB

AurumShield services corporate entities only.

All customers must undergo Know Your Business (KYB) verification.

Required Corporate Information

Customers must provide:

Legal entity name

Jurisdiction of incorporation

Business registration number

Principal place of business

Tax identification number

Nature of business activity

7. Beneficial Ownership Identification

Under FinCEN’s Customer Due Diligence Rule, AurumShield must identify beneficial owners.

A beneficial owner is defined as:

• Any individual owning ≥25% of the entity
• Any individual exercising significant control

Each beneficial owner must undergo identity verification.

8. Identity Verification Infrastructure

AurumShield integrates with Veriff for automated identity verification.

Veriff performs:

Government ID Authentication

Verification of government-issued identity documents.

Biometric Liveness Detection

Facial recognition and anti-spoofing verification.

Corporate Registry Verification

Automated validation against official corporate registries.

Beneficial Ownership Mapping

Extraction and verification of UBO ownership structures.

Verification sessions produce tamper-evident verification reports.

9. Sanctions Compliance

AurumShield enforces strict sanctions compliance.

All counterparties undergo screening against:

OFAC SDN List

OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List

EU Sanctions List

United Nations Sanctions List

10. Sanctions Screening Infrastructure

AurumShield integrates with OpenSanctions.

Screening occurs at three stages:

Onboarding Screening

All entities and UBOs screened before account creation.

Pre-Settlement Screening

Screening occurs before each settlement transaction.

Continuous Monitoring

Customers are periodically re-screened.

Matches trigger an automatic compliance review.

11. Digital Asset Risk Monitoring

Because AurumShield interacts with stablecoins, the system includes blockchain risk monitoring.

Controls include:

Wallet address risk scoring

Exposure detection to illicit wallets

Sanctions wallet screening

Transactions involving high-risk blockchain exposure are halted.

12. Transaction Monitoring

AurumShield maintains automated transaction monitoring systems.

The monitoring engine evaluates:

Structuring

Attempts to evade reporting thresholds through multiple smaller transactions.

Layering

Use of complex transaction patterns to obscure origin of funds.

Velocity Anomalies

Rapid movement of large funds across multiple counterparties.

Economic Purpose Analysis

Transactions inconsistent with a customer’s known business activity.

Suspicious patterns trigger automated compliance alerts.

13. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Under 31 CFR §1022.320, AurumShield files Suspicious Activity Reports when suspicious activity is detected.

SAR triggers include:

Suspected money laundering

Terrorist financing indicators

Structuring attempts

Fraudulent identity submissions

Sanctions evasion attempts

SARs must be filed within 30 days of detection.

14. Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)

CTR reporting is required under 31 CFR §1010.311.

A CTR must be filed for cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single business day.

Although AurumShield primarily processes electronic transfers, any cash-equivalent transaction exceeding this threshold must be reported.
