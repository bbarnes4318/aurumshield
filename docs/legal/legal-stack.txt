UCC ARTICLE 8 SECURITIES ENTITLEMENT STRUCTURE
1. Legal Characterization of Client Interests

The parties agree that Client’s ownership interest in bullion held through the AurumShield Network shall be treated as a securities entitlement within the meaning of Article 8 of the Uniform Commercial Code.

For purposes of Article 8:

• AurumShield shall act as Securities Intermediary
• Client shall act as Entitlement Holder

Client’s interest in bullion held through AurumShield shall constitute a financial asset credited to Client’s securities account.

2. Securities Account

AurumShield shall maintain for Client a Securities Account reflecting the quantity and type of bullion credited to Client.

Entries recorded in such account constitute the authoritative record of entitlement.

The Securities Account may contain:

• allocated bullion balances
• pending settlement transfers
• fractional bullion interests resulting from partial transfers

3. Entitlement Holder Rights

Client, as Entitlement Holder, shall possess the following rights under Article 8:

The right to direct AurumShield to transfer financial assets.

The right to receive payments or distributions relating to assets.

The right to obtain delivery of the underlying bullion.

These rights are subject to:

• outstanding settlement obligations
• collateral liens
• operational requirements of the AurumShield settlement engine.

4. Control

AurumShield maintains control of financial assets within the meaning of UCC §8-106 until such time as assets are transferred or withdrawn pursuant to Client instruction.

5. Priority of Interests

No third party may acquire a superior interest in Client’s financial assets except:

• a secured party holding a perfected security interest
• AurumShield in connection with settlement obligations

6. Protection from Adverse Claims

Under UCC §8-502, Client’s securities entitlement is protected from adverse claims except where Client has acted in bad faith.

7. Governing Law for Article 8

For purposes of Article 8 of the Uniform Commercial Code, the jurisdiction governing the Securities Account shall be the State of New York.

SCHEDULE C
SETTLEMENT WATERFALL MECHANICS

The AurumShield Network operates a deterministic Settlement Waterfall designed to ensure orderly completion of transactions.

1. Settlement Sequence

Each transaction proceeds through the following stages:

Stage 1 — Order Initiation

Client submits settlement instruction.

Stage 2 — Funding Confirmation

Payment value is confirmed through:

• Fedwire receipt
• blockchain settlement
• liquidity provider confirmation

Stage 3 — Pre-Settlement Validation

The AurumShield engine verifies:

• account balances
• compliance checks
• counterparty verification

Stage 4 — Asset Lock

Relevant bullion balances are temporarily reserved.

Stage 5 — DvP Execution

Delivery-versus-Payment settlement occurs simultaneously:

• bullion title transfers
• settlement value transfers

Stage 6 — Final Settlement

The transaction state becomes Finalized.

At this stage the transaction is irreversible.

2. Settlement Failure Handling

If a settlement step fails:

• assets revert to original accounts
• the transaction is cancelled

3. System Integrity

The AurumShield settlement engine is designed to prevent:

• partial settlements
• race conditions
• inconsistent ledger states

AURUMSHIELD BULLION TITLE TRANSFER PROTOCOL

Network: AurumShield Institutional Settlement Network
Operator: PVN LLC
Version: 1.0

1. PURPOSE

This Bullion Title Transfer Protocol (“Protocol”) establishes the legal and operational framework governing the transfer of ownership of allocated bullion within the AurumShield Network.

The Protocol ensures that all bullion transfers occur through a deterministic Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) mechanism ensuring simultaneous transfer of value and title.

2. LEGAL STRUCTURE OF TITLE

Bullion held through AurumShield is maintained in allocated form at approved vault providers.

Legal ownership of each bar is assigned to a specific participant within the AurumShield ledger.

Title to bullion exists simultaneously in two forms:

Physical Title Record
Maintained by the vault provider inventory system.

Digital Ownership Record
Maintained by the AurumShield settlement ledger.

The AurumShield ledger constitutes the authoritative operational record for settlement transfers.

3. BAR IDENTIFICATION

Each bullion bar is identified by the following attributes:

• serial number
• refiner
• weight
• purity
• vault location

These attributes form the unique bar identity record.

4. DIGITAL TITLE REPRESENTATION

For operational efficiency, AurumShield represents ownership of bullion bars through ledger-based digital title entries.

Each entry represents a participant’s entitlement to specific bullion bars stored in vault custody.

Digital title entries may represent:

• whole bars
• fractional bar interests

Fractional ownership may occur when bars are partially transferred between counterparties.

5. TRANSFER INITIATION

A bullion transfer is initiated when a participant submits a Settlement Instruction through the AurumShield platform.

The instruction must specify:

• counterparty identifier
• quantity of bullion
• settlement price
• settlement asset

6. PRE-SETTLEMENT VALIDATION

Before settlement execution, the AurumShield system verifies:

• sufficient bullion balance
• sufficient settlement funds
• regulatory compliance checks
• counterparty eligibility

If any validation fails, the transfer request is rejected.

7. ASSET LOCK

Upon successful validation, the AurumShield system temporarily reserves the relevant bullion balance within the participant’s account.

Reserved balances cannot be used for other transactions.

8. DELIVERY-VERSUS-PAYMENT EXECUTION

Settlement occurs through the AurumShield deterministic DvP state machine.

The state machine performs the following actions simultaneously:

Transfers digital title of bullion from seller to buyer.

Transfers settlement value from buyer to seller.

The settlement asset may include:

• fiat funds received via Fedwire
• digital settlement assets such as USDC or USDT.

9. FINALIZATION

Once the DvP process completes, the transaction enters the Finalized State.

At this point:

• ownership records are updated
• the transfer becomes irreversible
• vault inventory attribution is updated

No participant or administrator can reverse the transaction once finalized.

10. VAULT RECORD RECONCILIATION

Periodic reconciliation is performed between:

• AurumShield digital title records
• vault provider physical inventory records

This ensures consistency between digital and physical ownership records.

11. PHYSICAL DELIVERY

Participants may request physical withdrawal of bullion.

Such withdrawals require:

• bar identification
• vault release authorization
• logistics coordination

Physical delivery requests are processed by the vault provider.

12. GOVERNING LAW

Title transfers executed through the AurumShield ledger are governed by Article 8 of the Uniform Commercial Code and the laws of the State of New York.





AURUMSHIELD SETTLEMENT DEFAULT MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK

Network: AurumShield Institutional Settlement Network
Operator: PVN LLC

1. PURPOSE

This Settlement Default Management Framework establishes procedures for addressing participant failures to meet settlement obligations.

The framework ensures orderly resolution of failed settlements while protecting the integrity of the AurumShield Network.

2. DEFAULT EVENTS

A participant is considered in default if it fails to:

• deliver required settlement funds
• deliver required bullion balances
• meet collateral requirements
• comply with regulatory obligations

3. DEFAULT DETECTION

The AurumShield settlement engine continuously monitors participant accounts.

If a participant lacks sufficient funds or collateral to complete settlement, the system flags the account.

4. DEFAULT RESPONSE PROCEDURE

Upon detection of a default event, AurumShield may implement the following actions:

Step 1 — Transaction Suspension

Pending settlement transactions involving the defaulting participant are suspended.

Step 2 — Collateral Evaluation

AurumShield evaluates collateral held in the participant’s account.

Collateral may include:

• fiat balances
• allocated bullion

Step 3 — Collateral Liquidation

If necessary, AurumShield may liquidate collateral to cover outstanding settlement obligations.

Liquidation may occur through approved liquidity providers.

Step 4 — Network Protection

The defaulting participant may be temporarily or permanently removed from the network.

5. LOSS ALLOCATION

Losses resulting from a default are covered in the following order:

participant collateral

liquidation proceeds

recovery from the participant

AurumShield does not operate a mutualized loss pool.

Participants do not share losses from other participants’ defaults.

6. EMERGENCY MARKET CONDITIONS

In extraordinary market conditions, AurumShield may implement temporary measures including:

• settlement delays
• liquidity provider substitution
• trading suspensions

These measures are intended to maintain orderly market functioning.

7. NETWORK STABILITY

AurumShield reserves the right to take any action reasonably necessary to preserve system integrity.

Such actions may include:

• restricting participant activity
• adjusting collateral requirements
• suspending network operations.
