Section 2 — Claim Frequency Model

AurumShield indemnifies deterministic protocol breach risk, not commodity price fluctuations. Frequency is modeled using a Negative Binomial distribution to account for overdispersion inherent in cluster-risk environments (e.g., localized logistics failures or regional assay discrepancies).

P(N=k) = [Γ(k+r) / (k!Γ(r))] * (1-p)r * pk
  • Weighted Portfolio λ: E[N] = 0.0262 per transaction.
  • Expected Annual Claim Count: E[Nannual] = 3.14 claims.
  • Variance σ²: Modeled at 4.25, reflecting the strictly controlled, yet potentially correlated, nature of transit/escrow failures.

Overdispersion Justification

Operational cluster risk (Hub, Corridor, Assay coupling) introduces overdispersion. Target overdispersion ratio selected is VMR = 1.35. This results in a dispersion parameter r ≈ 9.0.

Section 3 — Claim Severity Model

Given the deterministic claims doctrine (Charter Part VI), a claim is either approved or denied based on binary logic (ClaimDecision). Partial losses are rare; if a protocol breach occurs, the severity typically approaches the exposure cap.

We model gross severity X using a Lognormal distribution bounded by the Transaction Limit (L) and modified by the Deductible (d).

Net = max(0, min(X, Limit) - Deductible)

Section 8 — Reinsurance Structure Modeling

To optimize the ECR and provide capital relief, we model the requested treaty structure over the Phase 1 portfolio.

Modeled Treaty

  • Quota Share (QS): 40% (Cedes 40% of premium and risk).
  • Excess of Loss (XoL): $5M xs $5M (Aggregate Layer). Protects against cluster events exceeding $5M in aggregate net retained losses.
  • Aggregate Stop Loss (ASL): 70% attachment point on earned premium.

Post-Treaty Metrics

  • Net Retained Expected Loss: $2,475,000 (after 40% QS).
  • Net Retained VaR (99%): Capped heavily by the $5M xs $5M aggregate XoL. New VaR 99% drops to $6,800,000.
  • Capital Relief: The structure frees up approximately $14,500,000 in risk capital.
  • ECR Improvement: Net ECR drops from ~6.0x to a highly conservative ~2.5x.

1. Capital Sufficiency & ECR

Based on the stochastic simulation of 120 annual transactions capped at $5M with a $25M capital base, AurumShield is adequately capitalized to withstand a 99% confidence level loss event ($18.25M VaR). However, without reinsurance, the platform operates near its structural capacity, relying heavily on the automated hardstops defined in Charter Section 4.15 to prevent the Exposure-to-Capital Ratio (ECR) from breaching systemic thresholds during cluster events.

2. Structural Strengths

The portfolio's greatest actuarial strength is its deterministic state machine. By conditioning indemnity strictly on the presence of cryptographic evidence and irreversible settlement (FUNDS_CONFIRMED_FINAL), AurumShield eliminates subjective adjudication. This converts what is traditionally "moral hazard" and "fraud risk" into quantifiable, binary operational failures.

3. Correlation and Concentration Vulnerabilities

The primary threat to the $25M capital base is correlation risk—specifically Hub Collusion and Tier 3 Corridor geopolitical seizure. The Gaussian Copula modeling indicates that if a primary verification Hub experiences a systemic failure, the probability of simultaneous multi-cap losses spikes dramatically. The modeled TVaR(99%) of $22.6M reflects this tail risk.

4. Reinsurance Necessity

To scale active exposure beyond the $150M cap and achieve a 1.3x economic capital buffer, treaty reinsurance is mathematically mandatory. The modeled structure (40% QS + $5M xs $5M Aggregate XoL) successfully truncates the tail risk, reducing TVaR(99%) to $8.1M and fundamentally protecting the balance sheet from catastrophic cluster correlation.
